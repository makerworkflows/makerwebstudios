## Part 1: Pre-Call State Management

**Before EVERY strategy call or meeting:**

### The 5-Minute Ritual
1. **Music** -- 2 minutes of high-energy music (your playlist, not Spotify radio)
2. **Movement** -- Stand up, move, stretch. Get blood flowing. You cannot sell sitting in a slump.
3. **Mirror Declaration** -- Look yourself in the eye and say: "I built this. I know manufacturing. I know digital. This person NEEDS what I have. It's a disservice not to sell them."
4. **Review their audit** -- Have their Digital Presence Score and 3 specific problems ready to cite
5. **Smile before you speak** -- They hear it in your voice even on a phone call

### Your State Checklist
- [ ] Energy: HIGH (not manic -- controlled fire)
- [ ] Conviction: Do I believe this will help them? (If no, don't take the call)
- [ ] Preparation: Do I know their business, their competitors, their pain?
- [ ] Certainty: Can I quote my price without flinching? (Practice the number out loud)

## Part 2: The MWS Sales Framework

### Phase 1: CONNECTION (First 30 Seconds)

**Goal:** Make them like you. Sound familiar, not formal.

**DO:**
- Use their first name immediately and naturally
- Reference something specific about their business (you just audited them -- use it)
- Sound like a fellow manufacturer, not a salesman

**DON'T:**
- "Hi, my name is Guillermo from Maker Web Studios, I'd like to..." (dead)
- Read from a script in monotone
- Jump straight into what you sell

**Example opener (cold outreach response):**
"Hey [Name], Guillermo here. I was just looking at [Company Name]'s Google listing and honestly -- for what you guys build, you deserve to be way more visible online. I've got some ideas. You got 5 minutes this week?"

**Example opener (strategy call):**
"[Name]! Good to finally talk. I've been going through your digital presence and I gotta tell you -- your operation is way more impressive than what the internet shows. Let me show you what I found."

### Phase 2: DIAGNOSIS (Minutes 2-10)

**Goal:** Make them feel understood. Identify pain. Let THEM tell you the problem.

**The 5 Discovery Questions:**

1. "How are most of your customers finding you right now?"
   *(Expected answer: referrals, word of mouth. This exposes the dependency.)*

2. "When someone Googles what you manufacture, do you show up?"
   *(Usually no. This creates awareness of the gap.)*

3. "What happens when a lead DOES come in -- who handles it, and how fast?"
   *(Exposes the sales process gap -- or lack of one.)*

4. "Who's your biggest competitor that you keep losing deals to?"
   *(Gets them emotionally invested -- now it's personal.)*

5. "If I could guarantee you one qualified inbound lead a month from your website, what would that be worth to your business?"
   *(Forces them to calculate the value themselves. A $50K contract = your $4,500 build is nothing.)*

**Key technique:** After each answer, PAUSE. Let the silence work. Then say: "That's exactly what I'm seeing." (Validates their experience + positions you as the expert who already diagnosed it.)

### Phase 3: PRESCRIPTION (Minutes 10-20)

**Goal:** Present the Revenue Engine System as the solution to everything they just told you.

**The Value Stack Presentation:**

"Based on what you just told me, here's what I'd build for you:"

1. **"First, the Revenue Engine Build"** -- Custom website designed specifically for manufacturers like you. Mobile-fast, SEO-optimized, with a lead capture system that works 24/7.
   *(Anchored value: $8,500)*

2. **"Second, I'm going to include the Texas Visibility Blueprint"** -- I'll audit your top 3 competitors, map out exactly what buyers are searching for your product, and optimize your Google Business Profile so you actually show up.
   *(Anchored value: $1,500 -- included free)*

3. **"Third, you get the Manufacturer's Sales Playbook"** -- Custom call scripts for your specific product, a lead response protocol so no inquiry goes cold, and the 3 objection crushers every manufacturer faces.
   *(Anchored value: $2,500 -- included free)*

4. **"And fourth, the 90-Day Revenue Launchpad"** -- A one-hour strategy session with me, your custom 90-day roadmap, and a KPI dashboard so you can see exactly what's working.
   *(Anchored value: $750 -- included free)*

**"Total value of everything I just described: over $13,000."**

**PAUSE. Let it land.**

**"Your investment: $4,500 to build it. $1,500 a month to grow it."**

**"And I guarantee at least one qualified inbound lead within 90 days -- or I keep working for free until you get one."**

### Phase 4: CLOSE (Minutes 20-25)

**Goal:** Get the commitment. Handle objections with conviction, not desperation.

**The Assumptive Close:**
"So here's what happens next -- I send you a simple agreement, you lock in your spot, and I start building within 48 hours. The sooner we launch, the sooner leads start coming in. Sound good?"

**Scarcity (honest):**
"I only take on 3 new Revenue Engine clients a month. I'm hands-on with every build -- I don't outsource to a junior dev in another country. I currently have [X] spots left for [month]. Once they're gone, next opening is [month+1]."

**Urgency (real):**
"Every month your competitors with better websites are capturing the searches you should be getting. That's not a scare tactic -- that's Google Analytics. The longer you wait, the more ground you give up."

## Part 3: Objection Scripts (MWS-Specific)

### "I need to think about it"
"Of course you do -- this is a real investment. Here's what I'd like to do: let me send you a quick breakdown of everything we discussed, with the exact numbers, so when you sit down tonight you actually have something concrete to review. Would that be fair?"

*(If they still hesitate):*
"Let me ask you this -- what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the price, the timing, or whether this will actually work? Because if it's whether it works, that's what the guarantee is for."

### "Your price is too high"
"I hear you. Let me ask you something though -- what's it costing you right now to NOT have a website that generates leads? If you're losing even one deal a month to a competitor who shows up online and you don't, that's $10K-$50K in revenue walking out the door. My entire year-one cost is less than one lost contract."

### "We already have a website / web guy"
"That's great -- it means you already understand you need to be online. The question is: is your current site generating inbound leads? Are you showing up on page 1 for what you manufacture? Because if not, you have a digital business card, not a revenue tool. And there's a massive difference."

### "I need to talk to my partner/spouse"
"Totally respect that. What do you think they're going to say? Usually it's 'what does it cost and what do we get?' -- and you already have both of those answers. If you want, I can jump on a quick call with both of you and walk through the numbers together. Would that help?"

### "We've been burned by agencies before"
"I hear that a LOT from manufacturers. And honestly, most agencies don't understand manufacturing -- they build pretty templates and disappear. I spent 16 years on the shop floor before I built a single website. I speak your language, not just marketing jargon. And that's why I back everything with a 90-day guarantee -- if it doesn't work, I work for free until it does."

### "Can you do it cheaper?"
"I could -- but then I'd have to cut something, and everything in the Revenue Engine exists for a reason. The visibility blueprint is what gets you found. The sales playbook is what closes what comes in. The retainer is what keeps you growing month over month. If I cut any of those, I can't guarantee results. And I'd rather guarantee results than give you a discount."

### "Send me a proposal / email me the details"
"I'll absolutely send you everything in writing. But the most valuable thing I can show you won't fit in a PDF -- it's the custom audit of your digital presence compared to your competitors. That takes 5 minutes on a call. When's good -- tomorrow or Thursday?"

### "We don't need a website right now"
"I get it -- you've been growing on referrals and relationships. The challenge is, referrals don't scale. There's a ceiling. And right now, when someone your sales team just met Googles your company name -- what do they see? Because I promise you, your competitors' websites are showing up. And in 2026, your website IS your first sales meeting."

### "Let me get back to you"
"No problem at all. Just so I can be prepared -- when you get back to me, what would make this a 'yes' for you? Is it seeing a case study, talking numbers, or just timing? That way I can have exactly what you need ready."

## Part 4: Follow-Up System

### The 48-Hour Rule
After EVERY strategy call, within 48 hours:
1. Send a personalized recap email (not a template)
2. Include: their specific problems, your proposed solution, pricing, guarantee
3. Include one competitor screenshot showing their competitor's online presence
4. End with a clear next step and specific date

### The Follow-Up Cadence
- Day 0: Strategy call
- Day 1: Recap email with proposal
- Day 3: "Quick question" text -- "Hey [Name], did you get a chance to look at the proposal? Any questions?"
- Day 7: Value-add follow-up -- send them a free insight about their SEO or a competitor move
- Day 14: Direct ask -- "Hey [Name], want to lock in your spot for [month]? I've got [X] left."
- Day 21: Break-up email -- "Hey [Name], haven't heard back so I'm going to assume the timing isn't right. If anything changes, you know where to find me. In the meantime, I'd keep an eye on [competitor] -- they just updated their site."

### The Break-Up Email Works Because:
- Loss aversion kicks in -- they don't want to lose the option
- It demonstrates you're not desperate -- you have other clients
- It leaves the door open with zero pressure
- 30% of "lost" deals respond to the break-up email

## Part 6: Pricing Confidence

### The Number: Say It Without Flinching

Practice this out loud, standing up, looking in a mirror:

"Your investment is forty-five hundred to build it and fifteen hundred a month to grow it."

Say it 10 times. Until it sounds like you're telling someone the weather.

### Why Premium Price is MORAL (Hormozi + Elliott):
- Cheap clients are the worst clients. They question everything, respect nothing, and churn fast.
- Premium clients commit. They do the work. They get results. They refer others.
- Your price is a FILTER, not a barrier. It filters for people who are serious.
- If you discount, you're telling them your work isn't worth what you said it was.
- Never compete on price. Stack more VALUE instead.

### The Friends Rate Exception
$1,500 build + $500/month. Only for 10+ year relationships. Never publicly listed. Never discussed in sales calls. This is a private gift, not a tier.
