## The 6-Prompt SEO System

### System Overview
Adapted from Alventra Marketing's proven local SEO framework. Each prompt leverages Claude Cowork's browser automation to gather competitive intelligence and create execution plans.

**Total Audit Time**: 2-3 hours (vs 30-40 hours manual)
**Output Format**: Organized spreadsheets with actionable next steps

---

### Prompt 1: Competitor Review Teardown

**Objective**: Analyze review velocity and identify gaps/opportunities

**The Prompt**:
```
Open Chrome and go to these competitor GBP listings: [URL1], [URL2], [URL3].
For each competitor, read their last 50 reviews. Extract:
- Total review count
- Average rating
- How many reviews in the last 30/60/90 days
- Most mentioned services in reviews
- Most mentioned neighborhoods or cities
- Recurring complaints

Then compare their review velocity to mine at [my GBP URL].
Output a spreadsheet with all this data and a separate tab that says exactly
how many reviews per month I need to catch the top competitor and how long it will take.
```

**What We Deliver to Client**:
- Review velocity comparison spreadsheet
- Gap analysis (how many reviews/month needed to catch competitors)
- Service keywords customers actually use (vs what business owner thinks)
- Competitor weakness analysis (recurring complaints = your angle)
- 6-12 month review generation roadmap

**Why This Matters**:
- Review velocity > total review count
- Google prioritizes recent, consistent reviews
- Identifies services customers actually talk about
- Reveals competitor vulnerabilities to exploit

---

### Prompt 2: Service Area Page Gap Analysis

**Objective**: Find missing service+location landing pages competitors have that you don't

**The Prompt**:
```
Open Chrome and crawl my website [URL] and these competitor sites: [URL1], [URL2], [URL3].
List every service+location landing page each site has.
Put it in a spreadsheet. Columns: service type, city/neighborhood,
my site (yes/no), competitor 1 (yes/no), competitor 2 (yes/no), competitor 3 (yes/no).
Highlight every combo where competitors have a page and I don't.

Then for my top 10 missing pages, open Google and search that keyword.
Note who's ranking in the top 5 and what their page covers.
I need to know what to build and what to beat.
```

**What We Deliver to Client**:
- Complete service+location matrix (you vs competitors)
- Prioritized page build list (top 10-20 gaps)
- SERP analysis for each target keyword (who ranks, what they cover)
- Content outlines for each missing page

**Why This Matters**:
- No landing page = invisible for that city/service combo
- Competitors ranking in 8 cities vs your 1 city = 8x the leads
- Systematic gap filling = predictable ranking improvements
- Most agencies charge $2K just for this audit

---

### Prompt 3: GBP Category Audit

**Objective**: Optimize Google Business Profile categories for maximum map pack visibility

**The Prompt**:
```
Open Chrome and go to Google Maps. Search '[service] in [city]' for these 3 keywords:
[keyword1], [keyword2], [keyword3].

For each search, note which of my competitors show up in the Map Pack.
Then open each competitor's GBP listing and extract their primary category
and all secondary categories. Put everything in a spreadsheet.

One tab per keyword. Columns: business name, primary category, secondary categories,
star rating, review count, ranking position.
Highlight any categories my competitors have that I'm missing from my GBP.
```

**What We Deliver to Client**:
- Category comparison matrix (you vs map pack competitors)
- Missing category recommendations
- Correlation analysis (which categories = which rankings)
- GBP optimization plan

**Why This Matters**:
- Categories directly influence map pack visibility
- Wrong/missing categories = invisible for high-intent searches
- Adding one category can unlock new search visibility overnight
- Most businesses set this once and never optimize

---

### Prompt 4: GBP Photo Audit

**Objective**: Build photo upload strategy for trust and ranking signals

**The Prompt**:
```
Open Chrome and go to my GBP listing at [URL] and these competitors: [URL1], [URL2], [URL3].
For each listing, count:
- Total photos
- Photos uploaded in the last 90 days
- Types of photos (team, jobs, before-after, office, trucks, equipment)
- Whether any look like stock photos

Put this in a spreadsheet comparing me vs each competitor.

Then give me a specific 8-week photo upload plan for my GBP.
Tell me exactly how many photos per week and what type to shoot.
Focus on before-afters, team on job sites, trucks in neighborhoods we serve,
and close-ups of completed installs. No generic office photos.
```

**What We Deliver to Client**:
- Photo inventory comparison (you vs competitors)
- 8-week photo upload calendar (what to shoot when)
- Photo type strategy (before-after, team, equipment, trucks in service areas)
- Execution checklist for consistent weekly uploads

**Why This Matters**:
- Google data: +42% direction requests, +35% website clicks
- Consistency > volume (weekly uploads beat one-time dumps)
- Photo types matter: before-afters = competence, team = trust, trucks in neighborhoods = service area signals
- Most businesses upload 10 photos once and forget

### Prompt 5: Citation Audit + NAP Cleanup

**Objective**: Fix NAP (Name, Address, Phone) inconsistencies across web directories

**The Prompt**:
```
Open Chrome and search for my business '[business name]' + '[city]' across Google.
Go through the first 10 pages of results and check every directory listing you find -
Yelp, BBB, Angi, Yellow Pages, Manta, Hotfrog, Apple Maps, Bing Places, Facebook,
and any others that come up.

For each listing log: directory name, listing URL, business name as shown,
address as shown, phone number as shown, website URL as shown.

Compare every listing against my correct info: [name], [address], [phone], [website].
Output a spreadsheet. Flag anything that doesn't match exactly,
even small differences like 'St' vs 'Street' or an old phone number.
Sort by highest authority directories first. That's my cleanup list.
```

**What We Deliver to Client**:
- Complete citation inventory (all directory listings found)
- NAP inconsistency report (every mismatch flagged)
- Prioritized cleanup list (high-authority directories first)
- Estimated ranking impact of cleanup

**Why This Matters**:
- NAP inconsistencies = Google trusts you less = lower rankings
- Most businesses have 20-50+ listings with errors (old phones, previous addresses, misspellings)
- Citation cleanup alone can move you 2-3 spots in map pack within 30 days
- Painfully tedious manually, automated with Claude

---

### Prompt 6: Backlink Gap Analysis

**Objective**: Find warm link building targets (sites linking to competitors but not you)

**The Prompt**:
```
Open Chrome and go to Ahrefs. Run a backlink analysis on my domain [URL]
and these 3 competitors: [URL1], [URL2], [URL3].

For each domain pull:
- Total referring domains
- Domain rating
- Total backlinks that have DR 40+
- Which pages get the most links

Then find every site that links to at least 2 of my competitors but not me.
Export everything into a spreadsheet.
Tab 1: my backlink profile vs competitors side by side.
Tab 2: sites linking to competitors but not me, sorted by domain authority.
```

**What We Deliver to Client**:
- Backlink profile comparison (you vs competitors)
- Warm outreach target list (sites linking to 2+ competitors but not you)
- Domain authority sorting (prioritize high-DR targets)
- Outreach email templates for each target type

**Why This Matters**:
- Backlinks = strongest off-page ranking factor (hasn't changed)
- Sites linking to 2+ competitors are warm targets (already link to businesses like yours)
- Conversion rates 5-10x higher than cold link building
- Tab 2 is a hitlist, not a guessing game
