## How It Works (3-step process)

### 1. Baseline
We run a comprehensive Lighthouse audit on your site -- Performance, SEO, Accessibility, and Best Practices. This is your starting line. Every number is recorded.

### 2. Autonomous Experiment Loop
Our system makes a targeted modification to your site's code, rebuilds it, and runs Lighthouse again. If the score improved, the change stays. If it didn't, it's rolled back instantly.

Then it does it again. And again. And again.

**100+ experiments. One night. Zero human error.**

Each experiment is logged: what was tried, what the score was, whether it was kept or discarded. Full transparency.

### 3. Delivery
You wake up to:
- Your optimized site with measurable before/after scores
- A complete experiment log showing every change that was tested
- A technical summary of what was kept and why
- Your site deployed with the winning configuration

## Objection Handling

**"Can't I just use PageSpeed Insights myself?"**
PageSpeed tells you what's wrong. It doesn't fix it. And it definitely doesn't run 100 experiments to find the optimal combination of fixes. Knowing your site is slow and making it fast are two different things.

**"My developer already optimized the site."**
Great -- then the baseline will be high and the improvements will be smaller. But in our experience, even "optimized" sites have 15-20 points of room. The autonomous loop finds things humans miss because it tests combinations, not just individual fixes.

**"How do I know you won't break my site?"**
Every experiment is committed to version control. If a change breaks anything, it's rolled back instantly -- before it ever touches your live site. You get the experiment log showing exactly what was tried and what was kept. Full transparency.

**"$1,500 for one night of work?"**
It's not one night of work. It's 100+ experiments that would take a developer 2-3 weeks to run manually. The system doesn't sleep, doesn't get distracted, and doesn't bill hourly. You're paying for the result, not the time.
