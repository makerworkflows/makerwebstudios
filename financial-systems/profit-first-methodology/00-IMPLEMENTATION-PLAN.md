# Profit First Implementation Plan for MWS

**MWS-Specific Execution Roadmap**

**Last Updated**: March 6, 2026
**Current Phase**: Phase 0 (Pre-Revenue)
**Next Trigger**: Day 1 of first revenue

---

## Executive Summary

This plan implements Profit First methodology for Maker Web Studios using a simplified 2-account model. It ensures **profitability from Day 1** by allocating profit BEFORE expenses, forcing constraint-driven innovation.

**Core Equation**: Sales - Profit = Expenses

**Target**: 8 retainer clients @ $1,500/mo = $12K MRR with 5% profit allocation by Month 3

---

## 2-Account Model (Simplified)

### Account 1: Business Checking (Mercury or Relay)

**Purpose**: Operating account - "live from this"

**Inflows**:
- Client payments (all revenue)

**Outflows**:
- Operating expenses (software, contractors, tools)
- Owner's pay (your salary/distributions)

**Access**: Debit card, checkbook, full access

---

### Account 2: Business Savings (Mercury or Relay)

**Purpose**: Reserves - "don't touch"

**Inflows**:
- Profit allocation (transfer 2x/month)
- Tax allocation (transfer 2x/month)

**Outflows**:
- Quarterly profit distributions (decision to reinvest or take)
- Tax payments (quarterly estimated, annual filing)

**Access**: No debit card (behavioral lock), manual transfers only

---

## Target Allocation Percentages (TAPs)

### Pre-$100K Stage ($0 - $12K MRR)

| Account | % | Monthly @ $12K MRR | Purpose |
|---------|---|-------------------|---------|
| **Profit** | 5% | $600 | Untouchable reserves, reinvest or distribute quarterly |
| **Tax** | 15% | $1,800 | Quarterly estimated taxes, annual filing |
| **Owner's Pay** | 50% | $6,000 | Your salary/distributions |
| **Operating** | 30% | $3,600 | Software, contractors, tools, marketing |

**Total**: 100% of revenue

---

### Scaling TAPs by Revenue Stage

| Revenue Stage | Profit | Tax | Owner's Pay | Operating |
|---------------|--------|-----|-------------|-----------|
| **$0 - $5K MRR** | 1% | 15% | 50% | 34% |
| **$5K - $12K MRR** | 3% | 15% | 50% | 32% |
| **$12K - $25K MRR** | 5% | 15% | 45% | 35% |
| **$25K - $100K MRR** | 10% | 20% | 40% | 30% |
| **$100K+ MRR** | 15% | 25% | 30% | 30% |

**Key Insight**: As revenue grows, profit % increases, owner's pay % decreases (absolute $ still increases), operating % stabilizes at 30%

### Phase 1: Day 1 of Revenue

**Trigger**: First client payment received (even if $500)

**Timeline**: Day 1

**Actions**:

1. **File LLC** (if not already done)
   - Use LegalZoom or Northwest Registered Agent ($300-500)
   - Texas LLC filing
   - Operating agreement (solo member)

2. **Get EIN**
   - IRS.gov (free, online, instant)
   - Required for business bank accounts

3. **Open Business Accounts**
   - Business Checking (Mercury or Relay)
   - Business Savings (same bank)
   - Link accounts for transfers

4. **Execute First Allocation**
   - Even if $500 revenue → 1% = $5 profit
   - Build the habit from Day 1
   - Transfer to savings immediately

**Output**: Legal entity, bank accounts, first allocation executed

**Success Criteria**:
- LLC filed and approved
- EIN received
- 2 bank accounts open and linked
- First profit allocation transferred to savings

---

### Phase 2: Week 1 (Recurring Transfers)

**Trigger**: Week 1 after first revenue

**Timeline**: Week 1

**Actions**:

1. **Set Up Recurring Transfers**
   - **1st of each month**: Transfer allocations (Profit + Tax)
   - **15th of each month**: Transfer allocations (Profit + Tax)
   - Automate via bank (if available) or calendar reminder

2. **Track Expenses**
   - All operating expenses from Business Checking only
   - Tag expenses in bank for categorization
   - Review weekly: "Can we do this for less?"

3. **Document Constraint-Driven Innovations**
   - Keep log of cost reductions forced by limited operating budget
   - Examples:
     - Manual Gmail instead of $500/mo Apollo.io (until revenue proves ROI)
     - 5 templates instead of custom design (speed + cost)
     - 6-prompt SEO instead of 30-hour manual audit (automation)

**Output**: Recurring transfer rhythm established, expense tracking started

**Success Criteria**:
- Transfers executed on 1st and 15th
- All expenses tracked in operating account
- At least 1 constraint-driven innovation documented

---

### Phase 3: Month 3 (Quarterly Review)

**Trigger**: End of Month 3 after first revenue

**Timeline**: Month 3

**Actions**:

1. **Increase Profit Allocation**
   - From 1% (Day 1) → 5% (Month 3)
   - Adjust TAPs based on actual revenue achieved
   - If below $5K MRR, stay at 3%
   - If above $12K MRR, move to 5%

2. **First Profit Distribution Decision**
   - Review savings balance (3 months of 1-5% allocations)
   - Options:
     - **Reinvest**: Leave in savings, build reserves
     - **Take**: Distribute to yourself (bonus/reward)
     - **Hybrid**: 50% reinvest, 50% take
   - Recommendation for Month 3: Reinvest (build safety net)

3. **Assess Methodology Effectiveness**
   - Review constraint-driven innovations log
   - Calculate savings vs. "unlimited budget" scenario
   - Adjust TAPs if needed based on actual operating costs

**Output**: Profit allocation increased, first distribution decision made, effectiveness assessed

**Success Criteria**:
- Profit allocation increased to target %
- Distribution decision made (document reasoning)
- Methodology assessment complete (keep, modify, or abandon)

---

### Phase 4: Month 12 (Annual Review)

**Trigger**: End of Month 12 after first revenue

**Timeline**: Month 12

**Actions**:

1. **Tax Payment from Tax Reserve**
   - Quarterly estimated taxes already paid (if applicable)
   - Annual filing (Form 1040 Schedule C for sole prop, or 1120S for S-Corp)
   - Pay balance due from tax savings account
   - Verify 15-20% allocation was sufficient

2. **Optimize TAPs for Year 2**
   - Review actual revenue vs. projected ($12K MRR target)
   - Adjust profit % based on revenue stage achieved
   - If scaled to team, consider expanding to 5-account model

3. **Annual Profit Distribution**
   - Review full year savings balance
   - Options:
     - **Reinvest**: Equipment, marketing, team
     - **Take**: Year-end bonus
     - **Hybrid**: 50/50 split
   - Recommendation for Year 1: 50% reinvest (growth), 50% take (reward)

**Output**: Year 1 complete, taxes paid, TAPs optimized for Year 2

**Success Criteria**:

## Constraint-Driven Innovation Examples

### What This Looks Like in Practice

**Scenario**: You have $3,600/month operating budget (30% of $12K MRR)

**Traditional Approach**: Spend up to $3,600 without constraint

**Profit First Approach**: "How can we achieve same results for $2,400?"

**MWS Examples Already Doing This**:

1. **Website Templates** (5 layouts)
   - Instead of: Custom design for every client ($40/hour × 10 hours = $400/site)
   - Profit First: Pre-designed templates ($0 per additional use)
   - Savings: $400/client × 8 clients = $3,200/month

2. **6-Prompt SEO System**
   - Instead of: Manual 30-hour audits ($50/hour = $1,500/audit)
   - Profit First: Automated Claude Cowork system ($200/month tools)
   - Savings: $1,300/audit × 4 audits/month = $5,200/month

3. **Manual Gmail Outreach**
   - Instead of: Apollo.io $500/month
   - Profit First: Gmail API free + manual work
   - Savings: $500/month (for now, upgrade when revenue proves ROI)

**New Innovations Forced by Limited Budget**:

4. **Contractor vs. Full-Time**
   - Instead of: Hire full-time developer ($6,000/month)
   - Profit First: Upwork contractor $50/hour × 20 hours = $1,000/month
   - Savings: $5,000/month (hire full-time at $25K+ MRR)

5. **DIY vs. Agency**
   - Instead of: Marketing agency $2,000/month
   - Profit First: DIY cold email + referrals ($0)
   - Savings: $2,000/month (consider agency at $50K+ MRR)

**Key Question to Ask**: "What would we do if we only had half the operating budget?"

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

### 1. "I'll start when revenue is consistent"

**Wrong**: Waiting until revenue is "stable" means you've already built bad habits

**Right**: Start with first $500 payment → $5 profit allocation. Build the muscle from Day 1.

---

### 2. "Borrowing" from profit/tax reserves

**Wrong**: "I'll just use the tax money for this expense and pay it back next month"

**Right**: No debit card on savings. Physical/mental barrier. Pretend it doesn't exist.

---

### 3. Increasing operating budget when revenue grows

**Wrong**: Revenue doubles → Operating budget doubles

**Right**: Revenue doubles → Operating budget increases slightly (30% → 35%), profit % increases significantly (5% → 10%)

---

### 4. Skipping transfers "just this once"

**Wrong**: "I'm too busy this week, I'll transfer next week"

**Right**: 1st and 15th are non-negotiable. 5-minute task, set calendar reminder with alarm.

---

### 5. Ignoring constraint-driven innovation

**Wrong**: "We have the budget, so let's spend it"

**Right**: "How can we achieve this for half the cost?" Challenge every expense.

## Emergency Procedures

### If Revenue Drops Below Projections

**Scenario**: Expected $12K MRR, actual is $6K MRR

**Actions**:
1. Maintain profit % (don't drop to 0%)
2. Reduce to 1-3% if needed (not 0%)
3. Cut operating expenses first (30% → 25%)
4. Owner's pay second (50% → 40%)
5. Document: What happened? How to prevent?

**Don't**: Raid profit/tax reserves

---

### If Operating Budget Insufficient

**Scenario**: Can't operate on 30% of revenue

**Actions**:
1. Review expenses line by line
2. Identify: Must-have vs. nice-to-have
3. Eliminate nice-to-haves completely
4. Negotiate must-haves (can you get 20% discount?)
5. Consider: Are we serving wrong clients? (low revenue, high cost)

**Don't**: Increase operating % beyond 35% (signals business model problem)

---

### If Tax Reserve Insufficient

**Scenario**: Tax bill exceeds 15% allocation

**Actions**:
1. Review: Was estimate wrong or did revenue surge late year?
2. One-time: Use profit reserve if absolutely necessary
3. Adjust: Increase tax % to 20-25% for next year
4. Consult: Accountant to optimize tax strategy (S-Corp election?)

**Don't**: Scramble for cash outside business accounts
