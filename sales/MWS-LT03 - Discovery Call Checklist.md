# MWS-LT03 - Discovery Call Checklist

**Goal: Qualify the lead and book the audit demo. 15-20 minutes max.**

---

## Before the Call (5 min)

- [ ] Pull up their website on your screen
- [ ] Run PageSpeed Insights (have the score ready)
- [ ] Check their Google Business Profile (do they have one? Reviews? Photos?)
- [ ] Quick Google search: "[company name] + [city]" -- where do they rank?
- [ ] Check LinkedIn for the person you're speaking with
- [ ] Open your notes -- have their name, company, industry, how they entered pipeline

---

## Opening (2 min)

- [ ] "Hey [name], appreciate you taking the time. I'll keep this quick."
- [ ] Establish context: remind them how you connected (email, call, referral)
- [ ] Set the frame: "I want to learn about your business, see if there's a fit, and if so, I'll run a free audit on your site and show you exactly what's going on. Sound good?"

---

## Discovery Questions (8-10 min)

Ask these in order. Listen more than you talk.

- [ ] "Tell me about [company]. What do you guys do and who's your ideal customer?"
- [ ] "How are you getting customers right now? Word of mouth, referrals, online?"
- [ ] "What role does your website play in that? Is it bringing in leads or is it more of a digital business card?"
- [ ] "When was the last time your website was updated or redesigned?"
- [ ] "If I could show you exactly what's costing you leads online right now, would that be useful?"

**Listen for pain signals:**
- [ ] "We get most business from word of mouth" = they have no online lead gen
- [ ] "Our website is old / we need to update it" = they already know
- [ ] "We tried [agency] before and it didn't work" = burned before, need trust
- [ ] "I don't know if we need a new website" = needs education, show the data

---

## Qualification (mental checklist, don't ask directly)

- [ ] Are they the decision maker? (Owner, CEO, President = yes)
- [ ] Do they have budget? (1-50 employees, established business = likely yes)
- [ ] Do they have a real problem? (Bad website, no leads, no SEO = yes)
- [ ] Is there urgency? (Losing to competitors, seasonal push, new location = yes)

**If qualified:** Move to booking the audit demo.
**If not qualified:** End gracefully. "Sounds like the timing might not be right. I'll send you some info and we can reconnect down the road."

---

## Transition to Audit (2 min)

- [ ] "Based on what you're telling me, I think there's a real opportunity here."
- [ ] "Here's what I'd like to do: I'll run a full audit on your site -- PageSpeed, SEO, Google presence, the whole picture. Takes me about 10 minutes to put together."
- [ ] "Then we'll hop on a quick call and I'll walk you through exactly what's working, what's not, and what it would take to fix it. No obligation."
- [ ] "When works better for you -- [Day A] or [Day B]?"

---

## Close the Call (1 min)

- [ ] Confirm date/time for audit demo call
- [ ] "I'll send you a calendar invite right after this."
- [ ] "Anything else on your mind before we wrap?"
- [ ] "Talk soon, [name]."

---

## After the Call (5 min)

- [ ] Send calendar invite immediately
- [ ] Send thank-you text or email within 1 hour:
  - "Hey [name], good chatting. I'll have your audit ready for [date]. Looking forward to showing you what I find."
- [ ] Update Google Sheets pipeline: move to "Audit Scheduled" stage
- [ ] Note key pain points and quotes from the call (use in proposal later)
- [ ] Begin running the audit (don't wait until the day before)

---

## Red Flags (Disqualify Early, Save Your Time)

- They want "just a quote" without talking -- price shoppers
- No decision-making authority and can't get the decision maker on a call
- Budget is clearly below your minimum ($0 budget, "maybe next year")
- They want to "think about it" before even seeing the audit -- low intent
- They bash every previous vendor -- they'll bash you too
