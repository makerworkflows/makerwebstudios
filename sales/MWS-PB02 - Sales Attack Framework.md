## The Pipeline

```
PROSPECT → QUALIFY → PROVE → CLOSE → UPSELL
 (find)    (call)   (audit)  (sign)  (expand)
```

Every day you are doing ALL of these simultaneously. You are not waiting for one phase to finish before starting the next. Pipeline compounds. Today's outreach is next month's revenue.

## The Math

To close 8 clients, work backwards:

| Stage | Conversion | Volume Needed |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Retainer clients signed | -- | **8** |
| Proposals sent | 25% close rate | **32** |
| Discovery calls booked | 50% get proposal | **64** |
| Positive replies | 50% book a call | **128** |
| Cold emails sent | 1-3% positive reply | **4,300 - 12,800** |

At 50-75 emails/day plus calls, LinkedIn, and referrals, that's **60-120 days of consistent outreach** to fill 8 slots.

Cold email is ONE channel. Stack all five and compress the timeline.

## Pricing Confidence

### Why $4,500 + $1,500/mo Is Right

**From the buyer's side:** A manufacturer with 20 employees does $2M-$10M annually. One new contract from the website = $50K-$500K in new business. $4,500 is a rounding error on the upside. Price is almost never the real objection when a deal dies.

**What you're actually selling:** Not a website. A lead generation asset that works while the owner sleeps, built by someone who spent 16 years on the floor of the industry he's building it for. You can walk into a metal fab shop and use the word "fixture" correctly. You know what a GMP audit feels like from the inside. No agency in the Valley has that. Most agencies in Dallas don't either. That's not a feature -- that's a moat.

**$1,500/mo reframe:** Less than one entry-level hire. No payroll taxes, workers comp, HR, sick days, turnover. Instead of a body filling a seat, they get a lead generation system running 24/7. Frame it exactly this way in proposals -- $1,500 stops feeling like an expense and starts feeling like the cheapest employee they've ever had.

**The sticker shock risk isn't the number** -- it's the absence of a clear ROI story. "$1,500/mo for SEO" sounds like a cost. "$1,500/mo to rank above your three closest competitors for every buyer searching for what you make in a 200-mile radius" sounds like an investment with a return.

**You are not your customer.** A manufacturing owner who cleared $800K in profit last year is not doing the same mental math you are when they see that number. Price it with confidence.

### You Don't Need Your Own SEO First

"I need to prove it on myself first" sounds responsible. It's actually a high-quality reason to delay starting. ABBA didn't hire you because your site ranked for "web design RGV." They hired you because you showed up with a 21-page audit more thorough than anything anyone had ever done for them.

Your own SEO matters at Month 6-12 for passive inbound. Right now your lead engine is cold email, cold calls, Google Maps, and referrals. None of those care about your domain authority.

## The Rules

1. Prospect EVERY day. Even when pipeline looks full. Especially then.
2. Nobody is coming to save you. The calls won't make themselves.
3. Speed to lead. Reply to interest within 1 hour. Every hour you wait, close rate drops 10x.
4. One CTA per interaction. Don't overwhelm. Move them ONE step forward.
5. Follow up until they buy or die. 7-11 touches average to close.
6. Track everything. If you can't measure it, you can't improve it.
7. The best time to prospect was yesterday. The second best time is right now.
