# MWS-LT05 - Proposal & Close Checklist

**Goal: Send a proposal they can't ignore. Handle objections. Get the signature.**

---

## Build the Proposal (send within 24 hours of audit demo)

### Structure

- [ ] **Page 1 -- Cover:** Their company name + "Web Strategy Proposal" + MWS branding
- [ ] **Page 2 -- The Problem:** 3-5 bullet summary of audit findings (use their data, not generic)
- [ ] **Page 3 -- The Solution:** What you'll build/fix, mapped to their specific problems
- [ ] **Page 4 -- Deliverables:** Exact list of what they get (pages, features, SEO, GBP, timeline)
- [ ] **Page 5 -- Investment:** Pricing with clear tiers (give them a choice, not a take-it-or-leave-it)
- [ ] **Page 6 -- Timeline:** Week-by-week breakdown of what happens after they sign
- [ ] **Page 7 -- Next Steps:** "Sign below" or "Reply to this email to get started"

### Pricing Options (always give 2-3 options)

Option 1 exists to make Option 2 look like a no-brainer. Price it high enough relative to Option 2 that almost nobody takes it. A website without SEO is a billboard in the desert -- don't make it easy for them to buy that.

- [ ] **Option 1 (Website Only):** One-time build, no SEO, no retainer. Priced at $5,000+. No ongoing support. They're on their own. This is the "I just want a pretty website that doesn't generate leads" option.
- [ ] **Option 2 (Website + Local Domination):** Build + 3-month SEO retainer. $4,500 build + $500/mo. This is the smart choice and where 80% should land. Bold this. Label it "Recommended."
- [ ] **Option 3 (Full Partnership):** Build + 6-month Market Leadership. $5,000 build + $1,500/mo. Premium. Anchors the other two and lands the occasional whale.
- [ ] Include monthly retainer pricing clearly -- this is how you get to 8 recurring clients
- [ ] The gap between Option 1 and Option 2 should feel irrational: "Why would I pay $5,000 for just a website when I can pay $4,500 + $500/mo and actually get leads?"

### Proposal Rules

- [ ] Reference THEIR audit data, not generic benefits
- [ ] Use their company name at least 5 times throughout
- [ ] Include competitor comparison screenshot from the audit
- [ ] Keep it under 7 pages -- respect their time
- [ ] No jargon. Write like you talk.
- [ ] Include a deadline: "This proposal is valid for 14 days"

---

## Send the Proposal

- [ ] Email subject: "Proposal for [Company Name] -- Maker Web Studios"
- [ ] Body: 3-4 sentences max. Don't re-explain the whole thing.
  - "Hey [name], here's the proposal based on our conversation and the audit findings."
  - "I included three options depending on how aggressive you want to go."
  - "Happy to jump on a quick call if you have questions. Otherwise, next step is on page 7."
- [ ] Attach PDF (not a link to Google Doc -- PDFs feel more professional and final)
- [ ] Send before 10am on a Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday (highest open rates)

---

## Follow-Up Sequence (if no response)

- [ ] **Day 2:** Text message -- "Hey [name], sent over the proposal yesterday. Let me know if you have any questions."
- [ ] **Day 4:** Email -- "Floating this back up. Happy to walk through any questions on a quick call."
- [ ] **Day 7:** Call them. Direct. "Hey [name], wanted to check in on the proposal. What are you thinking?"
- [ ] **Day 10:** Email -- "I know you're busy. Just want to make sure this doesn't fall through the cracks. The proposal expires [date]."
- [ ] **Day 14:** Final email -- "No worries if the timing isn't right. I'll keep your audit on file. When you're ready, I'm here."
- [ ] After Day 14: Move to "Nurture" list. Begin 90-day nurture sequence below.

---

## 90-Day Nurture Sequence (Post Day-14 Leads)

Most B2B sales at the $4,500+ level close on touch 5-9, not touch 1-4. This is where deals go to grow, not die. One useful piece of content per month. No pitch. Stay in their orbit until they're ready.

### Month 1 (Day 30)

- [ ] Email: Share a relevant insight about their industry, not about you
  - Example: "Hey [name], saw this stat -- 73% of manufacturers say their website doesn't generate leads (Thomas Industry survey). Reminded me of our conversation. Hope things are going well."
- [ ] No CTA. No pitch. Just value.

### Month 2 (Day 60)

- [ ] Email: Share a before/after case study or result from a similar client
  - Example: "Hey [name], just wrapped a project for a manufacturer in [city]. Took their site from a PageSpeed 28 to a 94 and they're getting 3x the contact form submissions. Thought you'd find it interesting."
- [ ] Soft CTA: "Let me know if you ever want to revisit what we talked about."

### Month 3 (Day 90)

- [ ] Email or call: Direct but respectful re-engagement
  - Example: "Hey [name], it's been about 3 months since we last talked. I still have your audit on file. If the timing is better now, I'd be happy to update it and put a fresh proposal together. If not, no worries at all."
- [ ] If no response after Day 90: move to long-term list (quarterly touch, holiday check-in)

### Rules

- [ ] Never pitch in Month 1. Earn the right to pitch by Month 3.
- [ ] Always reference your previous conversation -- they need to remember you.
- [ ] If they reply at any point, immediately re-enter the active pipeline.
- [ ] Track nurture leads separately in Google Sheets ("Nurture" tab).

## Handling Objections

### "It's too expensive"

- [ ] "I understand. Which option were you leaning toward?"
- [ ] "What's it costing you right now to NOT show up on Google? How many leads are going to [competitor] instead?"
- [ ] "Option 2 is where the value is. The website alone won't generate leads without SEO behind it. That's the whole point."
- [ ] Never discount. Add value instead (extra page, extended support, bonus GBP setup).

### "I need to think about it"

- [ ] "Totally fair. What specifically do you want to think through? Maybe I can help clarify."
- [ ] "Is there someone else who needs to weigh in on this?"
- [ ] "When would be a good time to reconnect -- this week or next?"
- [ ] Set a specific follow-up date. Vague = dead.

### "We tried an agency before and got burned"

- [ ] "What happened?" (Listen. Don't defend the industry.)
- [ ] "That's exactly why I walk you through the audit first -- so you see the data before spending a dollar."
- [ ] "I can start with a smaller scope so you can see results before committing to a retainer."

### "Can you just send me a quote?"

- [ ] "I could, but it wouldn't mean much without context. The 15-minute call lets me understand your business so the proposal actually makes sense for you. How's [Day] at [Time]?"
- [ ] If they insist: send a range, not a fixed price. "$3,500 - $7,500 depending on scope. Quick call will narrow that down."

### "I know a guy who can do it cheaper"

- [ ] "There's always someone cheaper. The question is whether they'll build something that actually brings in leads or just looks nice."
- [ ] "What did your current site cost? And how many leads has it generated?"
- [ ] Don't compete on price. Compete on results.

---

## Close the Deal

- [ ] Once they say yes: "Let's do it. I'll send the contract today."
- [ ] Send contract within 2 hours of verbal yes (momentum dies fast)
- [ ] Contract should include: scope, timeline, payment terms, what's NOT included
- [ ] Accept e-signature (DocuSign, HelloSign, or even a signed PDF reply)
- [ ] Collect deposit (50% upfront or first month retainer) before starting ANY work
- [ ] Send welcome email: "You're in. Here's what happens next..."

---

## After the Close

- [ ] Update pipeline: move to "Client - Active"
- [ ] Send onboarding questionnaire (logo files, brand colors, content, access credentials)
- [ ] Schedule kickoff call within 48 hours
- [ ] Add retainer to recurring revenue tracker
- [ ] Update your count: ___ / 8 clients
