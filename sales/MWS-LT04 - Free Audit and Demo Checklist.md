# MWS-LT04 - Free Audit & Demo Checklist

**Goal: Show them the problem with data. Make it undeniable. Then offer to fix it.**

---

## Build the Audit (before the demo call)

### PageSpeed & Technical

- [ ] Run Google PageSpeed Insights (mobile + desktop) -- screenshot both scores
- [ ] Note Core Web Vitals: LCP, FID/INP, CLS -- highlight any failures in red
- [ ] Check mobile responsiveness -- screenshot any broken layouts
- [ ] Check SSL certificate (HTTPS or not?)
- [ ] Check load time in seconds

### On-Page SEO

- [ ] Check page title tags (are they generic like "Home" or optimized?)
- [ ] Check meta descriptions (missing? Duplicate? Too long?)
- [ ] Check H1 heading structure (is there one? Does it include keywords?)
- [ ] Check image alt text (missing on most images = common)
- [ ] Check URL structure (clean slugs or messy parameters?)

### Google Presence

- [ ] Search "[company name] + [city]" -- where do they rank? Page 1? Page 3? Nowhere?
- [ ] Search their top service keyword + city -- do they show up?
- [ ] Check Google Business Profile: claimed? Optimized? Photos? Reviews? Posts?
- [ ] Count their Google reviews vs top 3 competitors
- [ ] Check NAP consistency (Name, Address, Phone match across web?)

### Competitor Comparison

- [ ] Run PageSpeed on their top 2 competitors -- screenshot scores side by side
- [ ] Compare Google review counts: them vs competitors
- [ ] Compare service area pages: how many do competitors have vs them?
- [ ] Note anything competitors do well that they're missing entirely

---

## Assemble the Audit Presentation

- [ ] Organize findings in a simple format (Google Doc, PDF, or screen share walkthrough)
- [ ] Lead with the most painful finding (PageSpeed score of 23? That's your opener)
- [ ] Include screenshots -- visual proof is more powerful than words
- [ ] Include competitor comparison -- nothing motivates like seeing rivals winning
- [ ] End with "Here's what it would take to fix this" (not a full proposal yet, just direction)

---

## Run the Demo Call (20-30 min)

### Opening (2 min)

- [ ] "Hey [name], I dug into your site and found some interesting stuff. Ready to take a look?"
- [ ] Share screen or send the audit doc

### Walk Through Findings (15 min)

- [ ] **Start with PageSpeed:** "Your site scores a [X] on mobile. Google considers anything under 50 a poor experience. Here's what that means for you -- visitors are bouncing before they even see your homepage."
- [ ] **Show the competitor comparison:** "Here's [competitor]. They score [X]. When someone searches for [service] in [city], Google is choosing them over you partly because of this."
- [ ] **Show Google presence:** "When I search [service + city], you're not showing up on page 1. [Competitor A] and [Competitor B] are. Here's why..."
- [ ] **Show the GBP gap:** "You have [X] reviews. Your top competitor has [Y]. Google uses review velocity as a ranking signal."
- [ ] **Show missing pages:** "Your competitors have landing pages for [service] in [city A], [city B], [city C]. You only have one general page. That's why they're showing up in those cities and you're not."

### Let Them React (3 min)

- [ ] Pause after the findings. Let them process.
- [ ] They'll usually say something like "I had no idea" or "That's frustrating"
- [ ] Validate: "Most businesses we audit are in a similar spot. The good news is these are all fixable."

### The Transition (5 min)

- [ ] "Here's what I'd recommend: [brief outline -- new site, SEO foundation, GBP optimization]"
- [ ] "I can put together a proposal with exactly what we'd do, the timeline, and the investment. Want me to send that over?"
- [ ] If they say yes: "I'll have it to you by [tomorrow/Friday]. What's the best email?"
- [ ] If they hesitate: "No pressure. Take a look at the audit, sit with it, and let me know if you want to see what the fix looks like."

---

## After the Demo (same day)

- [ ] Send the audit document to them via email
- [ ] Email subject: "Your website audit -- [Company Name]"
- [ ] Body: "Hey [name], great talking today. Here's the full audit we walked through. Let me know if you have any questions. I'll have the proposal ready by [date]."
- [ ] Update pipeline: move to "Proposal Stage"
- [ ] Begin building the proposal immediately

---

## Key Principles

- **Show, don't tell.** Screenshots and data beat opinions.
- **Competitor comparison is your best weapon.** Nobody wants to lose to their rival.
- **Don't pitch on this call.** Prove the problem. The proposal comes next.
- **If they're not shocked by the findings, the audit wasn't thorough enough.**
