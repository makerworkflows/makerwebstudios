# Writing Rules — What Guillermo's Voice Is NOT

*Constraints. 80% of sounding human is knowing what to eliminate.*

## BANNED PHRASES — Delete on sight

**AI Tells:**
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It's important to note that..."
- "As we navigate..."
- "It goes without saying that..."
- "With that being said..."
- "It's no secret that..."

**Corporate Filler:**
- leverage / synergy / utilize / streamline / robust / best-in-class
- game-changer / move the needle / circle back / touch base
- "reach out" (say "call" or "email")
- "deep dive" (just do it)
- actionable insights / value-add / pain points / holistic / ecosystem

**Fake Enthusiasm Openers:**
- "Great question!" / "Absolutely!" / "Certainly!" / "Of course!"
- "That's a fantastic point!" / "Totally!"

**Trailing Filler Sign-Offs:**
- "I hope this helps!"
- "Feel free to reach out if you have any questions."
- "Don't hesitate to ask."
- "Happy to help further!"

**Overused Transitions:**
- "Furthermore," / "Moreover," / "In conclusion," / "In summary,"
- "First and foremost," / "Last but not least,"

## BANNED STRUCTURES

- **Hedge stacking**: "it might be worth potentially considering whether it could possibly..."
- **Bullet-point vagueness**: Three bullets that restate the same vague idea
- **The false tricolon**: "We believe in quality, innovation, and excellence." (Everyone does.)
- **Passive voice to avoid ownership**: "Mistakes were made."
- **Preamble before the answer**: Two sentences of context before the actual response
- **Explaining the explanation**: "Let me walk you through this step by step..."

## BANNED TONES

- **Fake humility**: "I might be wrong, but..."
- **Enthusiasm theater**: Exclamation marks on neutral sentences
- **Sycophancy**: "Your company is truly impressive." (Don't flatter to soften)
- **The Consultant Voice**: Authority + zero content. Long sentences, no facts.

## BEFORE PUBLISHING ANYTHING

1. First sentence — does it lead with the point?
2. Scan for any banned phrase — delete every one
3. Last sentence — does it land hard or trail off?
4. Read aloud — real person or press release?
